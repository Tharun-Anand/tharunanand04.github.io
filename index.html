Research Interests

My research focuses on advancing computer vision through both traditional and deep learning-based algorithms to tackle imaging inverse problems. I aim to establish a research group dedicated to the mathematical analysis of learning-based image reconstruction algorithms and the development of real-time, state-of-the-art techniques in image and video enhancement.

Key research interests:

  • Learning-based Iterative Algorithms: Developing image restoration algorithms with provable convergence guarantees.
  • Real-time and Power-efficient AI: Creating AI solutions for video restoration that are both real-time and energy-efficient.
  • Deployable Generative Models: Designing generative models for practical image and video restoration applications.
  • Accelerating Diffusion Models: Enhancing the efficiency of diffusion models through rigorous mathematical analysis.

Background

Prior to my current role, I served as a Chief Engineer at the AI Video Processing Lab, Samsung Research Institute, Bengaluru. I earned my Ph.D. from the Department of Electrical Engineering at the Indian Institute of Science, Bengaluru, in 2022, under the supervision of Prof. Kunal Narayan Chaudhury. I completed my M.Sc. by Research in Electrical Engineering from the Indian Institute of Science in 2018, and my B.Tech in Electronics and Communication Engineering from the Amrita School of Engineering, Coimbatore. From 2011 to 2014, I worked as a software engineer at Pace Microtechnology, Bengaluru.

During my doctoral studies, I focused on developing provably convergent iterative solutions for image restoration using learning-based image regularization. My post-graduate research predominantly addressed approximating filtering mechanisms to enable fast image regularization.

As a research scientist at Samsung, I gained valuable insights into the challenges of deploying state-of-the-art computer vision algorithms in practical applications on edge devices. In the semiconductor industry, lightweight, computationally fast, and high-quality computer vision solutions are imperative.
